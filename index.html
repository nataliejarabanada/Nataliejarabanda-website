My name is Natalie Jarabanda and I am a first-year psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. However, I am originally from Los Angeles. I hope to graduate with a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology before going to graduate school for social psychology. My career goals include conducting research in social-cultural psychology to further the knowledge base about human behavior as it relates to our culture and surroundings. I have always been very interested in the reasons why us humans behave and think the way that we do. Next quarter, I am going to join UCR's Psychology Honor Society to meet more like-minded students within the field and to gain research experience here on campus and to be more involved in general.

I feel that I am a very passionate and determined student when it comes to my education and I hope this is portrayed through the various work projects I have done. For example, I interned at Children's Hospital Los Angeles for their brain imaging lab for a Concussion Study. It was associated with my high schools football teams and I was a great asset to the main researchers in inputing data and keeping the players in check. I also volunteered at USC Verdugo Hills hospital for a year in highschool where I managed front desk by answering phone calls, directed more than 50 visitors a day and handed out visitor passes, as well as discharging patients including elders and new mothers. I also volunteered at Mountain Avenue Elementary's Science Club where I oversaw up to 100 4th-6th grade students during presentations and assisted them with science activities, willingly advised and offered my knowledge to students about school and life in general and also gave a 20 minute presentation about the brain and concussions and designed an activity to teach students the importance of wearing a helmet.
